# Contributing to the Osy Matrix License

Thank you for your interest in stress-testing the OML. Because this is a legal document, contributions are handled slightly differently than standard software repositories.

## How to Contribute

### 1. Found a Legal Loophole? (Open an Issue)
If you are a lawyer, a compliance officer, or a developer who spotted a semantic loophole, a contradictory sentence, or a jurisdictional flaw, **please open an Issue first**. 
* Title it clearly: e.g., "Loophole: Section 5 definition of Derived AI Model"
* Explain how a corporate legal team might exploit it.

### 2. Proposing a Fix (Pull Requests)
If you have a concrete fix for a legal loophole:
1. Fork the repository.
2. Make your changes to `LICENSE.md`.
3. **Do not change the plain-English "Core Directives" summary** unless the legal meaning has fundamentally changed.
4. Open a Pull Request detailing the legal reasoning behind your change.

### 3. What We Are NOT Looking For
* **Making it "more permissive":** The OML is intentionally an aggressive, strong copyleft license. PRs attempting to turn it into an MIT/BSD-style permissive license will be closed.
* **Adding niche clauses:** We want to keep the OML as lean and readable as possible. Avoid adding hyper-specific boilerplate that belongs in a corporate EULA.

## The Goal
Our goal is to forge a bulletproof, community-reviewed legal shield for indie developers. Your expertise is highly valued!
